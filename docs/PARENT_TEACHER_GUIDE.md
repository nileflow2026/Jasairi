## 1. What Is JASIRI?

JASIRI is a learning app built specifically for children with Down syndrome, aged 4 to 18. It brings together educational games, stories, music, and creative art tools in a single, calm, easy-to-use experience.

The app adapts to each child — it notices what they enjoy, where they do well, and where they need more support. Then it quietly adjusts to give every child the right challenge at the right moment.

JASIRI does **not** replace therapists, teachers, or caregivers. It is a tool to extend learning moments into everyday life — during a quiet afternoon, a commute, or a session at home.

---

## 2. Who Is It For?

### Children (primary users)

Children use the app independently or alongside a caregiver. The interface is designed so a child can navigate it without needing to read — large pictures, colours, sounds, and simple shapes guide the way.

### Parents & Guardians

You create and manage your child's profile. You can check how your child is getting on, celebrate wins together, and share progress with teachers or therapists.

### Teachers & Special Education Professionals

You can create accounts linked to one or more learner profiles. Progress data and skill reports help you plan sessions and track development over time. Note: teacher and therapist accounts require email verification before accessing learner data.

## 3. Getting Started

### What you need

- An Android or iOS smartphone or tablet
- An internet connection for the first setup (the app works offline afterwards)
- An email address for your guardian account

### Download the app

Search for **JASIRI Learning** in the Google Play Store or Apple App Store and tap Install.

### First launch

When you open JASIRI for the first time, you will see:

1. **Welcome screen** — a warm, friendly introduction with the JASIRI mascot.
2. **Onboarding** — three short screens explaining what the app does (you can skip these if you have used the app before).
3. **Account setup** — create your guardian account or sign in.

---

## 4. Creating a Guardian Account

> Children do not need accounts. Only parents, caregivers, teachers, and therapists create accounts.

1. On the welcome screen, tap **Get Started**.
2. Tap **Create account**.
3. Choose your role: **Parent**, **Teacher**, **Therapist**, or **Caregiver**.
4. Enter your name, email address, and a password.

   Password requirements:
   - At least 8 characters
   - At least one uppercase letter
   - At least one number

5. Tap **Create account**.
6. You will be signed in automatically.

> **Teachers and therapists:** You will receive a verification email. Click the link in that email before you can access learner data.

### Signing in to an existing account

1. Tap **I already have an account**.
2. Enter your email and password.
3. Tap **Sign in**.

If you forget your password, tap **Forgot password** on the sign-in screen.

---

## 5. Adding a Child Profile

You can add up to 3 child profiles per account.

1. After signing in, tap **Add a child**.
2. Enter the child's first name and age.
3. Tap **Save profile**.

That is all. JASIRI does not need a surname, photo, or any medical information. The less we collect, the safer your child's data.

### Editing or deleting a child profile

1. On the profile selection screen, long-press a child's card.
2. Choose **Edit** to update the name or age.
3. Choose **Delete** to permanently remove the profile and all its data.

> Deleting a child profile cannot be undone.

---

## 6. Switching Between Profiles

JASIRI separates the **child view** (for the child to use) and the **guardian view** (for you to check progress).

### Handing the device to your child

1. On the profile selection screen, tap your child's card.
2. The app switches to the child's learning hub.
3. Your child can now explore freely — they cannot access account settings or other profiles.

### Returning to the guardian view

1. From any child screen, tap the guardian icon in the top corner.
2. You will be returned to the profile selection screen.
3. Tap **My Dashboard** to view progress reports.

## 8. Activities in Detail

### 🎮 Games

JASIRI's games are short, structured, and encouraging. Each game targets a specific skill:

| Game type | What it develops | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Memory matching | Short-term memory | Flip cards and find matching pairs |
| Pattern recognition | Logic, sequencing | Complete the pattern of shapes or colours |
| Picture-word matching | Communication, vocabulary | Match a picture to its word |
| Touch coordination | Fine motor skills | Tap, drag, and swipe targets |

**Difficulty adapts automatically.** If a child finds a game easy, it gently increases the challenge. If they are struggling, it steps back — no frustration, no failure messages.

**Sessions are short.** Most games are designed to be completed in 3–7 minutes to match shorter attention spans.

**Every attempt is celebrated.** The app rewards effort, not just correct answers. Completing a round — even with mistakes — earns encouraging feedback.

### 📚 Stories

Stories in JASIRI are read aloud by the app. The child follows along with large illustrated pages. Stories are:

- Short (5–10 pages)
- Illustrated with bold, clear images
- Narrated slowly and clearly
- Available offline

Tap any word on the page to hear it spoken individually.

### 🎵 Music

The music section contains songs and sing-along activities. Children can:

- Listen to songs and watch animated lyrics
- Tap along to the rhythm
- Explore different instruments and sounds

Music is one of the most powerful tools for language development and emotional regulation. Even a few minutes a day can make a difference.

### 🎨 Art Studio

The art studio is a free, open-ended space for creative expression. There are no wrong answers here.

Children can:

- **Draw freely** with their finger — choose from 8 colours and 3 brush sizes
- **Colour templates** — 20 pre-designed pictures to fill in
- **Save artwork** to the device gallery
- **Share** with a guardian or teacher

The interface uses very large controls so children with motor coordination challenges can use it comfortably.

## 9. Understanding Progress Reports

The guardian dashboard shows how your child is developing across five skill areas:

| Skill | What it measures |
|---|---|
| Memory | How well your child recalls and matches information |
| Language | Word recognition, picture-word connections |
| Motor Skills | Touch coordination, swipe accuracy |
| Creativity | Art studio engagement, creative variety |
| Focus | Session length and engagement duration |

### Reading the dashboard

**Skill bars** show progress from 0 to 100%. These are built from real session data — the more your child plays, the more accurate these become.

**Activity feed** shows recent sessions: what they played, when, and how long.

**Streak counter** shows how many days in a row your child has engaged with the app. Consistency matters more than duration — even 10 minutes daily builds habits.

**Recommendations** — the app surfaces 2–3 suggested activities based on recent performance. These are automatically updated.

### Sharing reports with professionals

To share a progress summary with a therapist or teacher:

1. Open the **Reports** screen from the guardian dashboard.
2. Tap **Share report**.
3. Choose how you want to share (email, messaging app, etc.).

The report is a plain-language summary — not raw data — designed to be useful in a therapy or school meeting.

## 10. Setting Learning Goals

You can set up to 5 active learning goals for each child. Goals help focus the AI recommendations and give you a clear target to track.

### Creating a goal

1. Open the **Goals** screen from the guardian dashboard.
2. Tap **Add goal**.
3. Choose a skill area (Memory, Language, Motor Skills, Creativity, Focus).
4. Set a target level (Basic, Developing, Confident).
5. Optionally add a note for context.
6. Tap **Save**.

The app will suggest activities that support the active goals.

### Reviewing goals

Goals automatically update as your child progresses. When a goal is reached, you will see a celebration screen. Review goals every 4–6 weeks and adjust them with your child's therapist or teacher.

## 11. Tips for Supporting Your Child

### Create a routine

Short, regular sessions are more effective than long, occasional ones. Try 10–15 minutes at a consistent time each day — after school, before dinner, or as a wind-down activity.

### Sit with them

For younger children or those just starting out, sitting alongside your child and narrating what they are doing ("you found the matching card!") amplifies the learning.

### Follow their lead

If your child gravitates to art over games, let them. The app tracks engagement across all activities. Intrinsic motivation is the most powerful learning driver.

### Celebrate effort, not results

Mirror the app's philosophy at home. "You kept trying, that's amazing" lands better than "you got it right." JASIRI is designed around this principle.

### Use the recommendations

The dashboard's suggested activities are based on your child's actual data. Try to include at least one recommended activity per week.

### Share with the school team

If your child has an IEP (Individual Education Plan) or learning support plan, share JASIRI progress reports at review meetings. The skill data can complement formal assessments.

## 12. Offline Mode

JASIRI is built for places where internet connections are unreliable.

**What works offline:**
- All games (already loaded)
- Stories (already loaded)
- Music (already loaded)
- Art studio
- Viewing saved artwork
- Viewing previously loaded progress data

**What requires internet:**
- Creating or updating child profiles
- Syncing new progress data to the guardian dashboard
- Uploading artwork to the cloud gallery

When the connection returns, JASIRI automatically syncs in the background. You do not need to do anything.

> If you see a small offline indicator at the top of the screen, the app is working from saved data. Everything your child does is still recorded and will sync when you are back online.

## 13. Accessibility Features

JASIRI is built around accessibility. These features are always active — there is nothing to switch on.

| Feature | What it does |
|---|---|
| Text-to-speech | Key screens and buttons speak their label aloud |
| Large touch targets | All interactive elements are large enough for a child's hand |
| Reduced motion support | If the device has reduced motion enabled in settings, JASIRI animations are simplified |
| High contrast | Colour choices meet accessibility contrast standards |
| Haptic feedback | The device vibrates gently when buttons are pressed, giving tactile confirmation |
| Audio reinforcement | Correct answers are rewarded with sound; incorrect answers get a gentle, non-judgmental tone |

### Adjusting device accessibility settings

For the best experience, consider enabling these in your device settings:

**Android:**  Settings → Accessibility → TalkBack (screen reader)

**iOS:**  Settings → Accessibility → VoiceOver (screen reader)

JASIRI is tested with both TalkBack and VoiceOver enabled.

## 14. Privacy & Safety

### Your child's safety comes first

- Children never create accounts or log in. Only guardians authenticate.
- The app does not contain any external links, advertisements, or in-app purchases.
- Children cannot contact other users or access the internet from within the app.

### What data is collected

| Data | Purpose | Stored where |
|---|---|---|
| Guardian name and email | Account creation and login | Appwrite Cloud (encrypted) |
| Child's first name and age | Personalisation | Appwrite Cloud |
| Game session results (score, time) | Progress tracking and AI personalisation | Appwrite Cloud |
| Artwork images | Gallery and sharing | Appwrite Cloud Storage |

We do **not** collect:
- Full names, addresses, or phone numbers of children
- Photos of children
- Location data
- Device identifiers for advertising

### Your data rights

You have the right to:
- **Access** all data held about you and your child (email us to request an export)
- **Correct** inaccurate information (editable in-app)
- **Delete** your account and all associated child data at any time (in-app, or email us)
- **Object** to processing — though this will require closing your account

### Data storage

Data is stored on Appwrite Cloud servers. We comply with the **Kenya Data Protection Act 2019** and follow **COPPA** (US Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) principles.

## 15. Frequently Asked Questions

**My child doesn't read yet. Can they still use the app?**

Yes. JASIRI is designed so children can navigate it without reading. Pictures, colours, sounds, and simple shapes guide the way. The text-to-speech feature reads labels aloud.

**How long should each session be?**

There is no minimum or maximum. Most children engage for 10–20 minutes naturally before moving on. The app does not push children to continue — sessions end whenever the child is ready. Short, daily sessions are better than long, infrequent ones.

**What age is the app suitable for?**

JASIRI is designed for children aged 4–18 with Down syndrome. Activities are presented at different levels to suit a wide developmental range.

**Can two children share a device?**

Yes. You can create up to 3 child profiles on one guardian account. Each profile keeps separate progress data. Simply switch profiles from the profile selection screen.

**The app is slow. What can I do?**

- Close other apps running in the background.
- Restart the device.
- Ensure at least 1 GB of free storage.
- If the problem continues, uninstall and reinstall the app (your data is safely stored in the cloud and will restore on login).

**Can my child's teacher also see the progress?**

Yes. Teachers and therapists can create their own guardian accounts. You share a child's progress by using the **Share report** feature in the Reports screen, or by having your child's teacher create an account and you both link to the same child profile (multi-guardian support). Contact support for multi-guardian linking in V1.

**Is the app safe for unsupervised use?**

Yes, for the in-app experience. JASIRI has no external links, no chat, no advertising, and no in-app purchases. However, we recommend periodic check-ins — not for safety, but to celebrate progress together.

**Will the app work in areas with poor internet?**

Yes. Core activities (games, stories, music, art) all work offline. Progress is saved locally and synced when internet is available.

**How do I delete my account?**

1. Go to **Settings** → **Account** → **Delete account**.
2. Confirm. This permanently deletes your guardian account and all child profiles and their associated data.
